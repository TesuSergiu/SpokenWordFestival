SEBASTIAN DORSET WINNER OF THE YEAR SPOKEN WORD PRICE!

Last night was this year's Spoken Word prizes awarded in a fully Studenterhus where there was a stand-up comedy on the program. So it was obvious also appropriate that the award should go to a comedian with a seductive style and one that has helped to develop Odense new talent through more open scenes on student house during the year.
Jury writes about Sebastian Dorset:
"The Award recipient has a linguistic tone that does not aim for the lowest common denominator, he is trusting his audience that they understand sentences where the word FUCK is not included in every other sentence. He trusting his audience intellectual capacity, and do not go for the easy solutions, the quick point or a light laugh. His comedy is characterized by the fact that he dare wonder implied decisions, he dares to address the problem, is not stingy with his opinions, even when it is not the light. And although he is making fun of others, so he possesses the wonderful ability to do it with respect, because he possesses the fool's main characteristic self-irony. "
Congratulations to Sebastian!

SPOKEN WORD FESTIVAL 2014

Spoken Word Festival takes place in 2014, d. 18-23rd August, six wordy days with the spoken word art in all forms: Storytelling, rap, song, oratory, poetry, music and rhythm and everything in between.
We are rooted in Odense's cultural life and encourage everyone to participate actively with everything you have and can do. We work with the city's cultural institutions, associations, companies and volunteers to organize the festival diversity of concerts, events and projects. The festival takes place at the same time and in collaboration with Hans Christian Andersen Festival.

SPOKEN WORD AWARD WINNERS




Spoken Word prize is awarded once a year in connection with the completion of the Spoken Word Festival in Odense.

The award is given to an artist who has made a special effort to disseminate, develop or challenge the spoken word art or an artist who offers an inspiring and innovative talent in spoken word art. It can be in the form of storytelling, rap, poetry, songwriting or one of the many expressions and genres, inspired by the spoken word.
The spoken word art is in the understanding an expression that has deep roots in adult education and the democratic formation, and it takes its inspiration from poetry, literature and theater. The spoken word art cultivates language aesthetic, rhythmic and playful form, finding in it through the way of amusement, communication and opinion.
The committee that selects the winner, consists of Chief Librarian Kent Forest, narrator Bent Hansen, Head of student house in Odense Anja Følleslev and Spoken Word Festival founder Storyteller Jens Peter Madsen.
The prize includes a check for 10,000 kr. Which is sponsored by the Hans Christian Andersen Festival in Odense

HIGHLIGHTS AND HISTORY




In 2010, there was nothing new in Odense. There were cultural and scattered events, and there were festivals of music and books, for play and learning, but something was missing: The spoken word under one umbrella, storytelling, stand-up comedy, poetry slam, jazz'n'poetry, lectures and rhetoric. It was what the Spoken Word Festival started with nine events over three days with the incredible variety.


This year, the festival also international guests in the form of both the Irish storyteller Niall de Burca, and the Canadian duo Ian Keteku and Brad Morden, who taught and entertained with poetry slam with musical accompaniment.

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *

The TV station Odense Looks Red covered the festival intensively in 2012, and they have a series of reports and footage available on their side. And if it's too confusing, cut also an excellent ten minute introduction and overview of the whole festival together, as can be seen right here.

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *

In 2013 took over Odense Student Management from the original initiator, storyteller Jens Peter Madsen. It also gave Odense a new cultural festival sponsored by several companies, Hans Christian Andersen Festival. That meant a change in date from November to August, but it still managed to stack a festival on the legs with bicycle poetry in the pedestrian streets, storytelling by children and adults, as well as visits from the other side of the Atlantic by poets / musicians Tom Kimmel and Andy Fite, and British storyteller Cat Weatherill.
