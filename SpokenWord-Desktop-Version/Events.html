Spoken Word prize is awarded once a year in connection with the completion of the Spoken Word Festival in Odense.

The award is given to an artist who has made a special effort to disseminate, develop or challenge the spoken word art or an artist who offers an inspiring and innovative talent in spoken word art. It can be in the form of storytelling, rap, poetry, songwriting or one of the many expressions and genres, inspired by the spoken word.

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
2014: Sebastian Dorset

The Award recipient has a linguistic tone that does not aim for the lowest common denominator, he is trusting his audience that they understand sentences where the word FUCK is not included in every other sentence. He trusting his audience intellectual capacity, and do not go for the easy solutions, the quick point or a light laugh.

* * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * * *
2013: Hans Laurens

His Laurens stands for quality and high ambitions in the development of the own narration while he has been able to meet and inspire storytelling many new practitioners with the recognition and support that makes everyone feel comfortable and develop in the narrative togetherness courses, festivals and workhops.
